01 — Why accounts die

A single line of copy can end the whole account.

Peptides sit in the healthcare and unapproved-substances category. The platforms don't slow-walk it — a violation can suspend the account on detection, and the domain can be flagged so even a fresh account on the same URL won't run. This is the wall most sellers hit.

◉ Uncertified seller

Health claims, then flatline

Benefit language on a product page, a before/after in an ad, a dosage in the copy — flagged, disapproved, then account-level suspension. Spend stops overnight.

◉ Compliance-first build

Reviewable, so it compounds

Research-use framing sitewide, COAs attached, no medical claims anywhere the crawler looks. Ads pass review, stay on, and the curve keeps climbing week over week.

RISK 01

Research-chemical classification

BPC-157, TB-500, Ipamorelin, CJC-1295 and similar are read as unapproved substances. Marketed for human use, they trigger policy conflict instantly.

RISK 02

Claims the crawler finds anywhere

Not just product pages — blog posts, meta descriptions, image alt text and testimonials. One stray "heals" or "boosts recovery" is enough.

RISK 03

Account and domain bans

Repeat violations suspend the account; the domain gets flagged. Reinstatement runs weeks to months and isn't guaranteed. It's a revenue emergency.

Compliance-first sites get disapproved far less, and when something does flag, we respond inside 24 hours so it never escalates to an account-level suspension.
It depends on your model. For prescription or FDA-approved peptides sold through a licensed clinic, LegitScript is effectively the gate for both Google and Meta. For research-use-only peptides, the durable path is rigorous RUO positioning plus careful account and merchant structure. We assess exactly which route fits your catalogue in the audit and guide the certification or vetting process end to end.
The honest answer: the eligible set is narrow and platform-dependent, and it shifts. Our scrub covers all of it, not just the obvious pages.
